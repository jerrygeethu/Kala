KALA Celebrates 11th Annual Day

In every sense the celebration of the 11th Annual Day of Kerala Arts and Literary Association (KALA) UK on 20th October 2007 was a truly remarkable and enriching experience that lingered for days in one’s mind. The event was held at the Parmiter’s School Auditorium in Watford. The occasion was graced by the Chief Guest Prof. Vishnu Narayanan Nambudiri, Guests of Honour Dr. George Gheverghese Joseph, Professor of Mathematics and author, Civic Ambassador and Chairperson of Newham, Mrs. Omana Gangadharan, the Kathakali artiste, Shri Kottakkal Sasidharan Nair, and the corporate entertainer, Shri Praveen Chulliparambil. On this occasion KALA honoured Prof Vishnu Narayanan Nambudiri by presenting him with the Life Time Achievement Award in recognition of his commendable contribution to Indian poetry, Vedic wisdom and heritage. The award was presented by KALA Patron, Dr. P. Sukumaran Nair in the presence of KALA President Mrs. Geetha Menon and other distinguished guests.

The Annual Day celebrations brought out much more than the usual splendour of artistic programmes through a unique musical presentation of poetry and dance and thought provoking and humorous sketches and skits. The Chief Guest, Prof. Vishnu Nambudiri, pointed out that the occasion provided a meeting ground for many eminent persons who had nurtured a wish for many years to meet the other personalities. Dr. George Gheverghese Joseph, the author of "The Crest of the Peacock: non-European Roots of Mathematics" and many other publications spoke of the great contributions that Kerala had made to mathematics several centuries ago, which has not been recognised by the western countries. Civic Ambassador Mrs. Omana Gangadharan spoke of her great satisfaction at being able to meet the eminent chief guest and other guests, and wished all success to KALA in its activities.

As far as entertainments went, there was a fabulous line up of programmes starting with a tableau titled “Incredible India”, ably performed by very young performers of KALA, colourfully depicting India’s diverse culture. This was followed by an impressive dance medley ‘East meets West’ choreographed and presented by the Youth members of KALA. KALA also paid tribute to the work of the late author and poet, Padmasree Dr. Ayyappa Paniker. Solo and well-remembered songs sung by Mrs. Susha Ashok Kumar and Praveen Gopinath added a hint of nostalgia to the evening.

The powerful and enchanting Kathakali presentation, “Krishnakatha”, by Shri Kottakkal Sasidhran Nair through the exquisite combination of Aangikam, Mudra, Abhiynayam, Sabdam and Sangeetam had the audience spellbound. The highlight of the evening was the dance drama, “Muthiyum Chozhiyum”, based on the poem by Padmasree ONV Kurup. It was a unique presentation, bringing to life an old rural folk art once common in some of Kerala’s villages and involving the participation of very talented KALA members. Dr. Mayadevi Kurup choreographed the dance drama.

The post-dinner programmes also caught the interest of the audience. A fusion of instrumental music with Manoj Siva on the tabla and Hari on the violin, with Dr. Sethumadhavan providing vocal support, created a magical experience for the audience. ‘Mukhamukham’ with Prof. Vishnu Narayanan Nambudiri and Dr. George Joseph was an occasion for sharing their insights and experiences with the audience. Dr. Manoj Kumar, Editor of KALA’s in-house magazine, the Palmleaf, handed the first copy of the Annual issue to Prof. Vishnu Narayanan Nambudiri who presented it to the Civic Ambassador, Mrs. Omana Gangadharan, for its release. The variety of this year’s entertainment did not end there - Mr. Praveen Chulliparambil captured the attention of both young and old alike with his show ‘The Sixth Sense’, by weaving a magical concoction of mentalism and illusion.     
- Murali Nair


KALA Sportsfest – December 2008

As promised, KALA took steps to chase away the winter blues by holding a Sportsfest on 9th December 2007 at the Ferry Sports Centre in Oxford. The activities and games for the day were ably organised by Dr Jose Mathews. KALA Literary Evening – May 2008 

For more than six years KALA has been hosting a literary event that has provided a forum to its members and guests alike to share and discuss the delights of books that they have come across. This year was no different. A sumptuous feast of words was hosted and co-ordinated by Mr Natarajan at KALA’s Literary Evening on Saturday 10th of May 2008 at the Heatherside Community Hall, Heatherside, Camberley. As always, the evening included an outstanding group of speakers, both young and old, speaking in Malayalam and English on a wide variety of books. What was a good evening became even a better one with time at the end for discussion and socialising.

Interspersed with anecdotes and literary facts relating to the various presentations, the event’s host, Mr Natarajan, ably introduced KALA’s fine line-up of speakers and bards. Two books - ‘The Inheritance of Loss’, the Booker prize winner of 2006 and ‘Q & A’ by Vikas Swarup, the book riding the heights of popular fiction - were introduced by Mr Ramachandran. Comparisons were made between the two books in relation to the time that went into the writing of each book and the way in which each was crafted. Sangeetha Venugopal’s preferred choice was Khalid Hosseini’s ‘The Kite Runner’, a book that was too good to put down and which provided a fascinating insight into the history of Afghanistan from the time of the Russian invasion. This year also saw several new faces at the podium – Dr Arun Kishore, who touched on the art of story-telling through linear narration and fabula, highlighted the works of numerous writers in English who have roots in Kerala. From amongst this list of prolific Malayalee writers, he singled out Anitha Nair and her recent achievement, ‘Mistress’ for a brief review. Mr Rajeev’s presentation of Wendy Moore’s well-researched and biographical work, ‘The Knife Man’ recounted the tale of the father of modern surgery, John Hunter. The book journeys back to a time of crude and old-fashioned medical practices that makes one appreciate the modern times we live in today.

Although not newcomers to the event, there was much focus on the speakers that brought variety to the programme. Mr. Manoj Siva’s forceful presentation of two short stories written in Malayalam and authored by him was heartfelt and moving. The first titled ‘Ayyappana njaannaaru?’ is a reflection of the perpetual quest for one’s identity and the second is a variation to the myth of Ahalya, told in Manoj’s inimitable style. Dr. Mohanachandran’s sonorous recital of Edasseri’s ‘Poothapaattu’, forty odd years after he first recited it in the company of Edasseri’s son, gave the poem a life of its own and immense beauty. Mr Jagan took a different tack altogether and chose to speak about a subject close to his heart that was based on current issues and an offshoot of modern society – the effects of increased use of plastic on the environment. He left us with a very strong and clear message to reuse and recycle plastic bags, and our responsibility for saving the environment.

As always, KALA’s youth put on a tremendous performance, reviewing a wide-range of titles. Eight year old Manasi’s clear and confident recital of Robert Louis Stevenson’s poem, ‘My Shadow’ held the audience spellbound. George Orwell’s well-known work, ‘The Animal Farm’, took new meaning for Shiva Krishna as it became evident that the story drew parallels from the Russian Revolution of 1917. Dahlin Mony’s favourite read was Robert Muchamore’s ‘Mad Dogs’, a story revolving around gang wars and espionage. Brother, Damien Mony, on the other hand, was into the vampire series by author Darren Shan and chose to review his book, ‘Cirque Du Freak’. Whitbread Award winner Hilary Mckay’s book, Saffy’s Angel’ was Pavna Venugopal’s choice for its simple and elegant tale of Saffron and her siblings. Anoop Jagan chose to speak about Willis Hall’s play ‘The Long and the Short and The Tall’, which follows the tale of seven British soldiers in the Malayan jungle during the Second World War. Except for Shiva, all the others were making their first appearance at the event.

Mr Natarajan took a few minutes to pay tribute to the poet, Shri Kadammanitta, by the rendition of one of his earlier poems. Through the various passages in Gandhiji’s ‘My Experiments with Truth’, Mr Narayanan Pillai stressed the need for Gandhian thought and ideals in today’s hectic lifestyle. Mrs Santha Krishnamurthy’s rendition of her poem titled ‘Onam’ held sweet and nostalgic memories of an Onam celebrated in India. Mrs Gowri Bai took the opportunity to speak about the changes to the Malayalam poetry scene over the years. Her impromptu and melodious recitations of verses from well-known poems brought across the beauty and differences in the writings of the great Malayalee poets. In an age when time does not stand still and communication is mainly through emails and phone calls, Mr Hrishi Marar’s presentation of Kalidasa’s epic work ‘Meghasandesam’ beautifully describes sublime and imaginative ways of communicating with one’s beloved during a time of separation. With the recitation of a modern poem by Shri Madhusoodhanan Nair titled ‘Aathame, Samsyayam, Krishna’, Dr Sukumaran Nair conveyed the need to face all hardships in life with fortitude and a smile as Shri Krishna was known to have done.

It was a very memorable evening with a lot being said that gave much stimulation to the brain. KALA’s Literary evening highlighted the gift of words and even more so the joy of sharing it with others.
Geetha Menon

KALA Summer Walk – July 2008

We barely had a summer this year and therefore it took KALA a certain amount of organisation and plenty of luck with the weather to have its annual summer outing without being rained on! The day out was on held on 19th July 2008 at Cassiobury Park in Watford, which was picked after much hunting around by Preethi and Shankar for an ideal spot. Although the day started on a gloomy note, the rain clouds were soon whisked away and all those who got together ended up enjoying glorious weather, wonderful company and delicious food. The day’s activities took the form of quiet strolls around the park in little groups, watching the canal boats negotiating locks, playing football and cricket on the green, a quick snooze in the shade of a tree, enjoying a game of dumb charades and singing to one’s heart’s content in competition! The youngsters also had a go in the paddling pools and mini train circuit, and dabbled with paints to get their impression of the day out on paper. Now we wait in anticipation for next summer and a good weather day for KALA’s summer walk....

KALA Gopi Memorial Cricket – August 2008

Once again on Bank Holiday Sunday on 24th August 2008, the Bishop’s Stotford College ground in Bishop Stotford was the venue for the KALA Gopi Memorial Cricket match. KALA was able to organise another successful event this year too with help from Shashi, Preethi, Ravi and Praveen, who once again organised facilities at the beautiful grounds at Bishops Stortford and all those who attended and helped prepare a wonderful 'Onasadya'. With a slightly later start than planned due to inclement weather, the tournament progressed with an air of enthusiasm and competitiveness, the youth team determined to win the match this year. The adult team batted first and set the target of 134 runs in 30 overs. The youth team took up the challenge and played strategically. In a thrilling and exciting second half the youth team reached the required target losing 7 wickets and went on to win the rolling trophy. The winning run was scored by Damian, who remained not out along with the team captain, Praveen. Tanoj, who opened the innings for the adult team took 46 runs and was declared 'Man of the Match'. The 'Champagne/Chocolate Moment' was presented to young Damian. The men’s cricket was followed by a ladies match with a lot of enthusiasm. Renu Radhakrishnan's team won this match and Thankam Ramachandran was declared 'Woman of the Match'.
