KALA Annual Day Celebrations - 20 October 2007

KALA Celebrates 11th Annual Day

In every sense the celebration of the 11th Annual Day of Kerala Arts and Literary Association (KALA) UK on 20th October 2007 was a truly remarkable and enriching experience that lingered for days in one’s mind. The event was held at the Parmiter’s School Auditorium in Watford. The occasion was graced by the Chief Guest Prof. Vishnu Narayanan Nambudiri, Guests of Honour Dr. George Gheverghese Joseph, Professor of Mathematics and author, Civic Ambassador and Chairperson of Newham, Mrs. Omana Gangadharan, the Kathakali artiste, Shri Kottakkal Sasidharan Nair, and the corporate entertainer, Shri Praveen Chulliparambil. On this occasion KALA honoured Prof Vishnu Narayanan Nambudiri by presenting him with the Life Time Achievement Award in recognition of his commendable contribution to Indian poetry, Vedic wisdom and heritage. The award was presented by KALA Patron, Dr. P. Sukumaran Nair in the presence of KALA President Mrs. Geetha Menon and other distinguished guests.

The Annual Day celebrations brought out much more than the usual splendour of artistic programmes through a unique musical presentation of poetry and dance and thought provoking and humorous sketches and skits. The Chief Guest, Prof. Vishnu Nambudiri, pointed out that the occasion provided a meeting ground for many eminent persons who had nurtured a wish for many years to meet the other personalities. Dr. George Gheverghese Joseph, the author of "The Crest of the Peacock: non-European Roots of Mathematics" and many other publications spoke of the great contributions that Kerala had made to mathematics several centuries ago, which has not been recognised by the western countries. Civic Ambassador Mrs. Omana Gangadharan spoke of her great satisfaction at being able to meet the eminent chief guest and other guests, and wished all success to KALA in its activities.

As far as entertainments went, there was a fabulous line up of programmes starting with a tableau titled “Incredible India”, ably performed by very young performers of KALA, colourfully depicting India’s diverse culture. This was followed by an impressive dance medley ‘East meets West’ choreographed and presented by the Youth members of KALA. KALA also paid tribute to the work of the late author and poet, Padmasree Dr. Ayyappa Paniker. Solo and well-remembered songs sung by Mrs. Susha Ashok Kumar and Praveen Gopinath added a hint of nostalgia to the evening.

The powerful and enchanting Kathakali presentation, “Krishnakatha”, by Shri Kottakkal Sasidhran Nair through the exquisite combination of Aangikam, Mudra, Abhiynayam, Sabdam and Sangeetam had the audience spellbound. The highlight of the evening was the dance drama, “Muthiyum Chozhiyum”, based on the poem by Padmasree ONV Kurup. It was a unique presentation, bringing to life an old rural folk art once common in some of Kerala’s villages and involving the participation of very talented KALA members. Dr. Mayadevi Kurup choreographed the dance drama.

The post-dinner programmes also caught the interest of the audience. A fusion of instrumental music with Manoj Siva on the tabla and Hari on the violin, with Dr. Sethumadhavan providing vocal support, created a magical experience for the audience. ‘Mukhamukham’ with Prof. Vishnu Narayanan Nambudiri and Dr. George Joseph was an occasion for sharing their insights and experiences with the audience. Dr. Manoj Kumar, Editor of KALA’s in-house magazine, the Palmleaf, handed the first copy of the Annual issue to Prof. Vishnu Narayanan Nambudiri who presented it to the Civic Ambassador, Mrs. Omana Gangadharan, for its release. The variety of this year’s entertainment did not end there - Mr. Praveen Chulliparambil captured the attention of both young and old alike with his show ‘The Sixth Sense’, by weaving a magical concoction of mentalism and illusion. 

Murali Nair
